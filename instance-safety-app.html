About

The Instance mobile safety app is designed to aid victims of stalking and features both short term and long term prevention methods unlike most safety apps that focus on one or the other. This project was created in response to watching a close friend endure a stalking experience for two years.

Context

According to a 2014 survey from Statistics Canada, stalking is defined as "repeated and unwanted attention that causes the victim to fear for their personal safety or for the safety of someone they know".

In the 5 years preceding the study, 2 million Canadians have experienced stalking before.

With the increase in smartphone usage, some turn to emergency safety apps as a form of short-term prevention. Victims of stalking may occasionally use these emergency safety apps but have little use of them when the moment is over. Many overlook long-term prevention methods such as making a safety plan or documenting their experience.

Problem

How can we reassure and empower victims of stalking in situations where they have no control over where and when they might be harassed?

1. Research

A 9 question survey was given to a mixed demographic with 45 participants.

Survey Results

Participants explain why they felt scared or anxious to walk in their neighbourhood before. Most felt worried late at night, during walks and when alone. Particularly when an incident had ocurred in their area before.

Participants reveal why they used a safety app/service before.

Participants suggest what features they think might be useful in a safety app.

Interview

The following text is a selection of questions and answers from a one-on-one interview with a participant who has been stalked before.

Q4. How easy was it to document the evidence needed to build a case?
"Fairly difficult as there was a lot of details I needed to include to prove there was a pattern in the way this stranger was following me. So I had to make note of every location I saw him, what time of day it was, what he was wearing, etc."

Q5. What kind of evidence is needed to build a case?
"A history of when and where I encountered this guy (with detailed locations, dates and times), any photos, call histories, emails or texts. Basically anything I could use to prove I had reason to fear this man. The problem is since he was a stranger, I didn’t get any emails or texts or calls. So I had to rely solely on my random encounters."

Q8. How did preparing a plan affect you mentally and emotionally?
"I was scared at the time doing it… but afterwards I felt more mentally prepared for the worst to happen. It gave me a sense of control."

Q9. How reliable do you think your memory is?
"My short term memory is pretty good but things start to get hazy after a few days have passed."

3. Ideation

Solution Proposal

done

To create an app designed for both short-term and long-term harassment prevention. This way users can simultaneously protect themselves in emergencies and be proactive in documenting their experiences to build a case when filing a legal report.

done

Based on the previous research, if 48% of Canadians between 15-34 have been stalked, and 88.1% own a smartphone and rarely leave their homes without it, a mobile app might be the most reasonable and accessible defense in the case of harassment.

done

Further, if among the 25% of those who have been stalked have filed for a restraining order, and found the process difficult due to the amount of evidence they needed to gather, a safety app that has additional documentation features on top of strictly emergency features might prove useful.

The name of the app defines its primary dual purposes: short-term prevention during what might happen in a moment and long-term prevention over the situation as a whole.
